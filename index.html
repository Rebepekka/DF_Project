The KOTILO project promotes waste sorting and recycling at the source by using data and gamification.



Sorting waste at the source is a key component of the circular economy, and the success of source sorting has a critical impact on the quality and volume of material flows used in circular economy processes.


Problem...?

Project Objectives



  1. To identify challenges and problem areas related to consumers' and households' waste sorting and separate collection, as well as to increase understanding of consumers' expectations, needs, and ideas for efficient and smooth waste sorting at the source.

  2. To design and develop service concepts and models that promote waste sorting at the source, utilizing various digital tools (including gamification, competitions, or reward mechanisms tied to recycling outcomes).

  3. To explore ways in which consumers and households can be encouraged and/or rewarded for increasing recycling rates through successful waste sorting.

  4. To investigate what new business models could be created to better utilize and develop the intermediary labor market in the processing of recyclable materials.

  5. To communicate and share information about the project's activities and results with households, businesses, authorities, the education sector, and other stakeholders in the region, and to engage them in the project work.
